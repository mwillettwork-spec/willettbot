Record a new script
Pick your hotkeys, click Start, then do the thing you want to automate — clicks, typing, and keystrokes are captured in real time. Press your end hotkey to finish, then review and save.
⚠ Privacy warning
WillettBot records every keystroke while recording is active — including anything you type into password, credit-card, or other personal-info fields. Do not record yourself logging into any website or app. Stop recording first, log in manually, then start a new recording from after the login screen.
Tip: for one-off values that change every run — codes, today's date, an amount — record up to that field, then in the editor add a Manual input step. WillettBot pauses, asks you, and types your answer where the cursor is. Nothing sensitive ever lives in the script file.
First time recording? macOS will ask for permission to watch your keystrokes and to control Finder / System Events / any apps you use — click OK on every prompt. If you miss one, you can re-enable it in System Settings → Privacy & Security → Automation. Without Automation permission the recorder only captures raw clicks, not app switches or file opens.Windows doesn't ask for any permissions — keystrokes and clicks are captured immediately. Just hit Start.On X11 the recorder captures clicks and keys directly. On Wayland, app-context detection (which app you switched to, which folder you opened) won't work and you'll just get raw click + key events.
Hotkeys are optional — the Start/Stop buttons below always work. Use Esc or Return if Fn keys don't fire on your Mac.
